# Meeting Brief — Section Reference

Every brief must include these 13 sections in this order.

---

## 1. At-a-Glance Table

A quick-reference table with:
- Contact name
- Title
- Company
- Email (if found)
- LinkedIn URL
- Meeting type
- Meeting occasion
- Prior touch (any prior meeting or event)
- ✅ flag if prior brief exists for this account
- Prepared by (user name + date)

---

## 2. Prior Research on This Account *(only if prior briefs exist in OneDrive)*

Surface inherited context from existing briefs:
- Who at EPAM has met this company before
- What was discussed / what was agreed
- Known Salesforce AEs on the account
- Open opportunities or follow-up threads
- Recommendation: "Speak to [EPAM colleague] before this meeting for a warm handoff"

If no prior briefs: skip this section or note "First brief for this account."

---

## 3. Company Snapshot

- What the company does (1 sentence)
- Size, revenue, geography
- Recent news (last 6 months): funding, acquisitions, leadership changes, product launches
- Business model signals relevant to Salesforce / EPAM
- Technology signals: known tech stack, cloud usage, digital transformation initiatives

---

## 4. Contact Profile

- Career arc: prior roles, tenure, trajectory
- Why they're in their current role (if known)
- What drives them professionally (from LinkedIn activity, public interviews)
- Communication style signals (direct, analytical, relationship-first, etc.)
- Education
- Location

---

## 5. Salesforce Ecosystem Signals

- Which Salesforce clouds are in use or under evaluation
- Known Salesforce implementation partners / SIs at this account
- AppExchange presence (if ISV/partner)
- Salesforce event participation (Dreamforce, World Tour, etc.)
- Opportunity gaps where EPAM can add value

---

## 6. EPAM Relevance

- Why this conversation matters for EPAM's Salesforce Practice
- Most relevant EPAM capabilities for this account (e.g., Data Cloud, Service Cloud, AI, CPQ)
- Prior EPAM work in this industry vertical
- EPAM's competitive positioning for this account

---

## 7. Your Connection

- Any prior shared events, meetings, or introductions
- Mutual contacts (EPAM colleagues, Salesforce AEs, industry peers)
- Personal connection angles: shared education, geography, interests, career path parallels
- Suggested opening line for the meeting

---

## 8. Talking Points (3–5, numbered)

Each talking point:
- Headline (bold, 1 sentence)
- 2–3 sentence elaboration specific to this contact and company
- Must connect EPAM's capabilities to a real business problem this person faces
- Avoid generic: "EPAM is a top Salesforce partner" — be specific and commercial

---

## 9. Questions to Ask

5 high-value questions that:
- Are open-ended (not yes/no)
- Advance the deal or deepen the relationship
- Reveal pain points, priorities, or decision-making context
- Are phrased conversationally, not like an RFP

---

## 10. How to Make an Impression

Two parts:
- **Professionally**: what to lead with, what to avoid, how to frame EPAM's value for this persona
- **Personally**: tone, style, personal angles — based on what you know about this person

---

## 11. Objection Handling

A table with 3 likely objections and specific responses:
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| [specific to this account] | [concrete, non-defensive reply] |

---

## 12. Suggested Next Steps

3 specific, actionable next steps to close the meeting on:
- What to ask for at the end of the meeting
- Who else to involve from EPAM
- What to send as follow-up

---

## 13. Mutual Connections & Warm Introduction Paths

This section identifies people in your network who already know this contact or this company — and can help you walk in warmer.

Structure it as three tiers:

### Tier 1 — Direct warm intros (people you both know)
List any individuals confirmed as mutual connections:
- Name, their role/company, how you know them
- How they connect to the contact (colleague, former employer, industry peer, Salesforce ecosystem)
- Suggested action: *"Ask [Name] for a quick intro message before the meeting"*

### Tier 2 — EPAM colleagues with account or industry exposure
List EPAM people who have worked with this company or in this vertical:
- Name, role, basis of connection (found via Outlook, Teams, Delivery Central, prior brief)
- What they can provide: account intelligence, a warm reference, context on the contact
- Suggested action: *"Ping [Name] on Teams — they worked with [Company] on [project]"*

### Tier 3 — Salesforce ecosystem connections
List any Salesforce AEs, SEs, or partner reps who cover this account or have a relationship with the contact:
- Name (if identifiable), role, basis of connection
- Suggested action: *"Loop in [Name] — they own the Salesforce relationship at [Company]"*

### If no mutual connections are found
State clearly: *"No direct mutual connections identified. Consider requesting a LinkedIn introduction from [closest second-degree connection], or use the Salesforce AE relationship as your warm path in."*

**Format tip:** Present as a scannable list, not a wall of text. The user should be able to glance at this section and immediately know who to call before the meeting.
